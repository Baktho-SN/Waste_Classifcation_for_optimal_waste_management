Organic Waste

Biodegradable, Compostable, Nutrient-rich.

Organic waste encompasses biodegradable materials from living organisms, including kitchen scraps, yard debris, and agricultural residues. Its biodegradability makes proper disposal crucial to prevent environmental issues such as pollution and greenhouse gas emissions. Sustainable waste management practices, such as composting and anaerobic digestion, transform organic waste into valuable resources like nutrient-rich compost and biogas. Addressing organic waste aligns with global efforts to combat climate change and promote a circular economy. Regulations, community engagement, and public awareness contribute to fostering responsible organic waste disposal and advancing sustainable practices.

Recyclable Waste

Reusable, Reprocessable, Eco-friendly.

Non-biodegradable or recyclable waste consists of materials that do not naturally decompose and can persist in the environment for extended periods. Common examples include plastics, metals, glass, and certain chemicals. The management of non-biodegradable waste is vital for mitigating pollution and conserving resources. Recycling plays a central role in diverting these materials from landfills, reducing energy consumption, and lessening the demand for raw materials. Proper waste disposal methods, such as recycling programs and waste-to-energy processes, contribute to minimizing environmental impacts. Legislative measures, technological innovations, and public education campaigns are essential components in fostering sustainable practices and ensuring the responsible handling of non-biodegradable or recyclable waste.

What's the need to seperate
Organic and Recyclable waste?

Separating organic and recyclable waste is crucial for effective waste management. Organic waste, such as food scraps and yard waste, can be composted to create nutrient-rich soil, supporting sustainable agriculture. On the other hand, recyclable materials like paper, plastics, and metals can be processed and reused, conserving valuable resources and reducing environmental impact. By segregating these two types of waste, we streamline recycling efforts, promote resource conservation, and contribute to a cleaner, more sustainable environment.

Thus we have created a model that takes in images of a waste and classifies it as either organic or recyclable!
